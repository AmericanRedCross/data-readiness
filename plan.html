Plan your learning experience

Set your goals

Now that you’ve considered your priorities from both a program and diagnostic perspective, the next step is to create a roadmap for the team. To do so, consider your prioritized competencies and you may want to focus on those rated lowest, aiming to move all competencies to a level 2 or 3.

Review the learning objectives

Under each competency are several sub-competencies and indicators. These may also be considered the “learning objectives” as you create your roadmap. To be able to move up in your competency, all sub-competencies should be met. For example, if you want to prioritize “primary data collection,” you may want to move toward level 2 or 3 across all its sub-competencies.

Write your curriculum

The next step is to consider how your team will learn and practice the competencies. You will need to consider a number of factors (time, number of people, synchronous vs. asynchronous learning, remote vs. in-class sessions, etc.) to plan how your team will learn. In particular, consider, which resources, teaching tools, activities, and assessments you will need.

Find the resources you need

There are plenty of existing resources on meeting the different data readiness competencies. As a starting point, the toolkit has already identified resources from the IFRC, National Societies, and outside organizations to help you get started in the resources tab.

About

The data readiness toolkit is a roadmap and resource for the IFRC and National Societies to meet their data readiness goals.
