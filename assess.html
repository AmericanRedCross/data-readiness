Assess & Plan

Assess your team

The assessment and planning phase aims to help you move toward your data readiness goals. The data framework guides you through the main components of data readiness in humanitarian response and programs alongside a maturity matrix. Using this resource, you can identify where your team falls in terms of data readiness and learn what it means to increase your capacity in those domains.

Go to Framework

Using the framework

There is no "right" way to work through the framework. However, the following gives you the main steps to help you be successful in this journey. Read the descriptions and tips below as you work through the assessment and planning phase.

Identify your team

Once you have a sense of what is in the framework, the next step is to identify your team. Who should be included as part of the assessment will depend on your goals, but generally, you'll want to identify a project team or department.

For example, you can identify a team working on a specific project (such as a community surveillance project) or a team/department that regularly works with data (such as the cash assistance team). Make sure you include people responsible for the different components in the data readiness framework.

Reflect and score

With your team, go through the framework and rate yourselves from 1 (does not exist) to 4 (high performance). Use the descriptions to rate yourselves as objectively as possible. You may find belong in two categories; if this happens, choose the level that represents your team most of the time.

As you score yourselves, note why you chose that level. This can help you in the planning phase as you prioritize which areas to work on.

Review results and prioritize

Review your results and identify which areas you want to focus on. This stage takes a bit of thought and creativity -- which components are essential to your project and/or department? Where is there the most room for improvement? Some additional things you may want to consider are broader organizational strategy, funding opportunities, time constraints, and how transferable the competencies are to other teams/departments.

If you need additional help getting started, consider focusing on the sample competencies in the learn page.

About

The data readiness toolkit is a roadmap and resource for the IFRC and National Societies to meet their data readiness goals.
