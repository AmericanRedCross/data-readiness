Assess your priorities

Overview

The assessment phase aims to help you identify your data readiness goals. The data framework guides you through the data readiness competencies in humanitarian response and programs alongside a maturity matrix. Using this resource, you can identify where your team falls in terms of data readiness and learn what it means to increase your capacity in those domains.

Go to Framework

Recommended steps

There is no "right" way to identify your teams' priorities. There are many consideraitons, including broader strategic initiatives, project needs, time limitations, and funding opportunities. This section outlines the main steps to help you be successful in this journey. Read the descriptions and tips below as you work through the assessment and planning phase.

1. Select your team

The first part of the assessment is to identify the appropriate people to be involved in this diagnostic. Who should be included as part of the assessment will depend on your goals, but generally, you'll want to identify a project team or department.

For example, you can identify a team working on a specific project (such as a community surveillance project) or a team/department that regularly works with data (such as the PMER department).

2. Assess your team

With your team, go through the framework and rate yourselves from 1 (does not exist) to 4 (high performance). Use the descriptions to rate yourselves as objectively as possible. You may find you belong in two categories; if this happens, choose the level that represents your team most of the time.

As you score yourselves, note why you chose that level. This can help you in the planning phase as you prioritize which areas to work on.

3. Identify which competencies to prioritize

Depending on the purpose of promoting data readiness, there may be critical competencies needed for a specific project or strategic initiative. Review your results and identify which areas you want to focus on.

This stage takes a bit of thought and creativity -- which components are essential to your project and/or department? Where is there the most room for improvement? Some additional things you may want to consider are broader organizational strategy, funding opportunities, time constraints, and how transferable the competencies are to other teams/departments.

About

The data readiness toolkit is a roadmap and resource for the IFRC and National Societies to meet their data readiness goals.
