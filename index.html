An overview of data readiness

In the Red Cross Red Crescent Movement context, data readiness is the ability of National Societies to use quality and timely information to influence humanitarian operations and programs.

Why it matters

The IFRC and National Societies are thinking critically about data and how to leverage it better to improve our work. Through data readiness, organizations will be better equipped to respond to, recover from, and prepare for disasters.

How to get there

There are many paths to becoming a data ready organization, but this document lays out an easy-to-follow road map to data readiness. Whether you are learning about data readiness for the first time, developing a strategy for your team, or putting together a project proposal, follow the steps below and get started.


Learn the basics

Learn more about data readiness and how it applies to humanitarian operations and programs.


Assess your priorities

Using this assessment framework, you can identify where your team falls in terms of data readiness, learn what it means to increase your capacity in those domains, and identify where your team needs to improve.


Plan your learning experience

With your assessment and learnings, plan how your team will meet your data readiness goals. Use the resources here to get started.
